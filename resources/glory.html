God's Glory

(download sermon)

The revelation of God's power, His nature, His presence and sometimes accompanied by physical phenomena.

A revelation is an invitation - God inviting you to be part of something
God's Calling "Having the eyes of your understanding being enlightened; that ye may know what is the hope of his calling, and what the riches of the glory of his inheritance in the saints," Ephesians 1:18 Do we know the hope to which God has called us?
God Reveal His Glory Through His People "And hath put all things under his feet, and gave him to be the head over all things to the church, which is his body, the fulness of him that filleth all in all." Ephesians 1:22-23

The condition of the church today in general is not as should be as described in this text. We see Jesus taking blows because of the lifestyle of the church.

God has raised Christ above all things for the benefit of the church. This is why the church has a very important role to release the fullness of Christ to the community.
Raised Up Together "And hath raised us up together, and made us sit together in heavenly places in Christ Jesus: That in the ages to come he might shew the exceeding riches of his grace in his kindness toward us through Christ Jesus." Ephesians 2:6 This is where Jesus wants the church to be.
Manifestation of the Spirit "But the manifestation of the Spirit is given to every man to profit withal." 1 Corinthians 12:7 The spiritual gifts are the manifestation of the Holy Spirit. But, who is the Holy Spirit manifesting?
Manifestation of Christ "Howbeit when he, the Spirit of truth, is come, he will guide you into all truth: for he shall not speak of himself; but whatsoever he shall hear, that shall he speak: and he will shew you things to come. He shall glorify me: for he shall receive of mine, and shall shew it unto you." John 16:13-14 Here we can see that the Holy Spirit is manifesting Christ. The spiritual gifts are therefore a revelation of Christ. It's the revelation of His work, His nature, His character. The gifts are thus part of God's glory to be made known to mankind.

 Desire the Gifts

 The Apostle Paul asks us to eagerly desire the gifts. By desiring the gifts, we desire the fulness of Christ to be made known. "But covet earnestly the best gifts: and yet shew I unto you a more excellent way." 1 Corinthians 12:31
