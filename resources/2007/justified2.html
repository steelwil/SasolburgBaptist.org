The acquittal, the vindication or declaration of being right.

Justified Through Christ
Not by Works

"What shall we say then that Abraham our father, as pertaining to the flesh, hath found? For if Abraham were justified by works, he hath whereof to glory; but not before God. For what saith the scripture? Abraham believed God, and it was counted unto him for righteousness." Romans 4:1-4

 Abraham received justification because he believed in God. The same principle applies to us. We receive the righteousness of God by faith.

Justification Not Disserved

"Justification is 'legal'. It does not make us righteous, it declares us to be righteous." Martin Lloyd Jones

"But to him that worketh not, but believeth on him that justifieth the ungodly, his faith is counted for righteousness."   Romans 4:5

Forget the Past

 Being justified by God means that we have been acquitted and vindicated of everything in our past. If we truly repent of our sins then anyone who judges us because of our past, is in reality not judging us but Christ. "Therefore being justified by faith, we have peace with God through our Lord Jesus Christ:" Romans 5:1
 

Raised for Us

 "Who was delivered for our offences, and was raised again for our justification." Romans 4:25 Without the resurrection there is no justification. It's therefore difficult to separate the teaching on justification from the teaching on the resurrection since they go hand in hand. We are only justified if we believe that Christ was raised from the dead for our sins. This is one of the principles of Christianity.
