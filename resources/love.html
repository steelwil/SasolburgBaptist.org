Jealousy

(download sermon)

A strong feeling of possessiveness often cause by the possibility of something that belongs to one or ought to belong to one is about to be taken away. A passionate commitment to something which rightfully belongs to one.

God's Love and Jealousy Cannot be Separated
A Lover First "Greater love hath no man than this, that a man lay down his life for his friends." John 15:13 The most powerful warrior in battle is one who loves much. To lay down your life for your friends is a sign of a warrior, but even more than this, it's the sign of a lover. Anything we do to help other people will not be effective if we are not lovers first.
Love Never Fails "Love never faileth: but whether there be prophecies, they shall be done away; whether there be tongues, they shall cease; whether there be knowledge, it shall be done away." 1 Corinthians 13:7 This scripture shows the power of love. Can we even begin to imagine just how powerful God's love must be?!
God is a Jealous God We belong to Christ because we've been bought by the blood of Christ. Since we belong to Christ, He is passionately committed to us. So when the world is trying to direct our hearts away from God, He will intervene out of His jealousy. God's jealousy is therefore to our benefit since it's our protection. "Thus saith Jehovah of hosts: I am jealous for Zion with great jealousy, and I am jealous for her with great wrath." Zachariah 8:2

Into God's Love

 "And the Lord direct your hearts into the love of God, and into the patience of Christ." One of the things about the sinful nature is that our hearts will give its affections to so many things. God then steps in and directs our hearts into His love.

Created to Love

 "And he said unto him, Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy mind. This is the great and first commandment. And a second like unto it is this, Thou shalt love thy neighbor as thyself." Matthew 22:37-39 We were created to BE loved. We were also created to GIVE OUT love. However, very often we go astray looking for substitutes for this love in worldly things.

Loving the World

 "Love not the world, neither the things that are in the world. If any man love the world, the love of the Father is not in him." 1 John 2:15 If our heart is divided to the world, then we've removed ourselves from the position of knowing the love of God.
God's Love Completes Us "that he would grant you, according to the riches of his glory, that ye may be strengthened with power through his Spirit in the inward man; that Christ may dwell in your hearts through faith; to the end that ye, being rooted and grounded in love, may be strong to apprehend with all the saints what is the breadth and length and height and depth, and to know the love of Christ which passeth knowledge, that ye may be filled unto all the fulness of God." Ephesians 3:16-19 The revelation of God's love makes us complete even though we are not complete.
