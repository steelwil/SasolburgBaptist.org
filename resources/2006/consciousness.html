Consciousness

An alert cognitive state in which you are aware of yourself and your situation

It's inside of me
He said it and it happened

"Then he said to the tree, 'May no one ever eat fruit from you again.' And his disciples heard him say it." Mark 11:14

"Peter remembered and said to Jesus, 'Rabbi, look! The fig tree you cursed has withered!" Mark 11:21

 Jesus said something and it happened. The power of His voice called things into being that was not yet in existance. He believed in what He said!

Putting it into practice

 "I was ashamed to ask the king for soldiers and horsemen to protect us from enemies on the road, because we had told the king, 'The gracious hand of our God is on everyone who looks to him, but his great anger is against all who forsake him.' " Ezra 8:32 Everyone has a seed of faith that God placed inside of us. All we need to do is to speak it out. In the book of Ezra we see how the Church is a pilar of truth - putting their faith into practice.

And He will come through

 "...The hand of our God was on us, and he protected us from enemies and bandits along the way." Ezra 8:31 When we put our faith in God and trust Him with our most valuable posessions, like Ezra did, or like Abraham did with his own son Isaac, then He will deliver.
SPEAK it out! Faith comes from hearing the Word - even hearing your own words as you SPEAK it.  

Speak positive about YOURSELF

 "Do you not know that your body is a temple of the Holy Spirit, who is in you, whom you have received from God?..." 1 Corinthians 6:19 You might not love yourself, but you need to speak positively about yourself. You need to tell yourself: "This body of mine is a temple for the Sovereign Lord!"

Believe in yourself

 It's easier for us to say "the Spirit of the Lord is upon you", rather than "the Spirit is upon ME". This is very often due to doubt and unbelief we have in our hearts. We often don't see ourselves as being anointed, because we don't believe that we are.  
Don't be arrogant "After that, he poured water into a basin and began to wash his disciples feet, trying them with the towel that was wrapped around him." John 13:5 We're not saying that you have to be arrogant, all we're saying is that you need to know (and believe) who you are. Jesus knew who He was, and yet He showed no arrogance.
